Our subject and generative research focused on understanding the financial sphere and the complexity of trader’s lives, particularly in institutional trading.

Research Overview

Liquidnet is a dark pool brokeage that serves institutional traders. Our client approached us with the following prompt.

Problem

How do we create stickiness with traders through technology in an ever-changing landscape?

Focus Area

How can we provide insightful analytics and make recommendation based on trader’s decision making style?

We planned out the project following the double diamond process. The first diamond is about exploring the problem space with an open mind, and identifying user need.

Methods

Our primary research method is stakeholder interview. And due to different purpose and restraints, we did some of the interviews in a semi-structured way. We did collaborative creating with our interviewees. We also did an analogous domain research with the method called contextual inquiry.

22 Interviews
8 Collaborative Creating
3 Contextual Inquiries in Analogous Domain

We did several rounds of interview, and talked with 22 people in total. Some of them are from inside Liquidnet and some of them does not.

We did collaborative creating to understand stakeholders, customer journeys, and traders' screen usage. We build the design deliverable and bring them to our interviewees to critique to resolve compliance issue as well as to maximize efficiency.

We did analogous domain research with online poker players. We convinced ourselves the similarity by finding out that students in CMU poker club will become professional trader in the future, and they were even ask to play online poker as a training process in their trading internships. So we did contextual inquiries with them to understand their mentality, thus infer traders' mentality and emotional need.

Insights

Based on our consolidation, we concluded these 5 insights along with several opportunities we foresee.

01
Traders need to face a lot of risk, and they deal with it by being calm and confident.

"Ideally, I should always be calm."

-- Trader

"Being confident is crucial for being a good trader."

-- Protfolio Manger

Opportunities

Provide emotional support for traders.

Increase self-awareness around emotional balance, thus to avoid performance influenced by bad emotion.

02
Trader want to be unique.

"Generating unique insights keeps me at the top of the game."

-- Quant Trader

“I need to tailor my tools for my specific need and generate unique insight.”

-- Trader

Opportunities

Provide flexible tools to help to generate unique insights.

Provide personal report to help traders do a better job.

03
Trading is learning by doing and reviewing.

"At the end of the day, I'll review my performance"

-- Trader

"I want to validate my strategy with people that I trust."

-- Protfolio Manger, Ray

Opportunities

In post-trade review, show what famous traders would do.

Provide a more efficient post-trade summary.

04
Traders need to build trust for technology gradually.

"I need to know how it works before using them."

-- Trader

"I only know it works after using it for a while"

-- Portfolio Manger

Opportunities

Show traders the history performance of the algos.

05
Traders need to consume and use information efficiently.

"I need to consume information efficiently"

-- Trader

"I need to communicate with my team efficiently to reach consensus"

-- Portfolio Manger

Opportunities

Provide a clearer information architecture and visualization.

Support team communication by quickly share realtime information etc.

Research Synthesis

After carefully analyzing the institutional trading industry, our users (pimarily traders), and the Liquidnet product, we reached a point where we started synthesizing all this data. We redefined the original prompt and used brainstorming activities to uncover our opportunity areas.

Compiling Insights

To synthesize our user research data, as a team, we ran through three main exercises to gather all of our insights, figure out reoccurring problems, and rapidly create high level solutions.

Walking the Wall
20 Questions
Reverse Assumptions

We gathered our product model, stakeholder map, affinity diagrams, and  user interview notes together and posted them on a board to look through all at once. Then, we took some time to create new insights and re-emphasized the most important user needs.

Example User Need Focuses:

Quality of Professional Network: I want my leads to come from an exclusive source.
Data Analytics & Visualization: I need to be able to customize my work station.
Workflow: To do research, I need to be on multiple platforms which is taxing.

We generated questions that tried to answer the problems we found through walking the wall. The main goal here was to try to be specific enough to answer the user needs we identified, but also broad enough to create a whole design solution around it.

Example Questions:

How can we keep traders mentally alert to optimize their productivity?
How can traders be willing to revise their routines without sacrificing efficiency?
How can we design something visually articulate but familiar to traders?
How can we help traders better tackle work-life balance/avoid burnout?

We made assumptions based on our users’ needs and reversed each one. Then, we used this reversal to brainstorm a new service or feature offering. This set up the stage for preliminary solution exploration.

Prioritizing Our Problem Areas

To sum up our most important problem areas and user needs, we created 15 How Might We... statements that highlight each individual issue we found and propose a solution based focus. We plotted these onto a matrix and considered Liquidnet’s feasibility of building the potential product and also how much value this product could bring to our client. We ended up choosing 5 major questions we wanted to solve below:

HMW get traders to participate in making the product better?

HMW keep traders mentally alert to optimize their productivity?

HMW automate the right analytics fields to visualize data better?

HMW reorient traders with where they left off at work after a hiatus?

HMW get traders to avoid snowballing when they’re at a disadvantage?

Takeaways

Our biggest takeaways from the research phase of this project, was that we realized traders are habitual creatures and it is hard to convince them to change outright. In order for us to create a successful design solution, we need to think outside the box.  
‍
Also during this phase, we realized that due to compliance issues and information protection, we were not able to access the required data for us to properly analyze deep user behaviors. Therefore, we went back to the drawing board and reframed the proposal with focus on creating better front end components that Liquidnet desires. This will still include the data component as we can provide suggestions of how the client could collect data for our design in the future to gain better access to common user behaviors.

Original Prompt

Build a framework for Liquidnet using ML that analyzes user behavior to produce customized recommendations for trading actions and explore data-driven design to anticpate user needs.

Focus Area

Help Liquidnet transition further into the fintech space by designing a more user-oriented front-end with new customizable components and better data visualizations.
