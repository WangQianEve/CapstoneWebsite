Ideation
Our team onboarded into the financial sphere using generative research. We studied the traders' day and their work environment, including interactions with software. From this work, we understood traders' needs and desires from Liquidnet's software.
Overview
In this phase, we collected information about our users and "dark-pool institutional trading", which Liquidnet specializes in. Our work focused on understanding the trader (our user) and their technology (the Liquidnet 5 product).
Problem

We need to understand the trader's daily interactions, both good and bad, with Liquidnet.

Challenge

Our team had to understand Liquidnet 5 well enough level to design for Liquidnet’s future, without knowing every detail of it.

Research Journey
The team began learning about the average trader's lifestyle and technology usage. Once we had a better understanding of a traders, we studied Liquidnet's software, Liquidnet 5. Finally, we examined the current possible features and actions within Liquidnet 5.

Product
With a better understanding of traders' work, our team studied the Liquidnet software. We learned about the target customers and the features marketed towards them. Then, we compared the target to the actual users of Liquidnet.
Customers
An entire company or a single trader may purchase Liquidnet for their trading teams. When interacting wtih the customer, Liquidnet must keep the customer’s portfolio in mind.
Features
Liquidnet has acquired analytics technology to build upon its order management and execution software. They want their customers to do more than execute trades on Liquidnet.
Users
The actual users of Liquidnet often avoid the front-end product and features. Traders want tools to be easily learned and their value apparent, not hidden behind a stream of actions.
How do we know about the product?
RESEARCH METHODS
We are a group of 5 Carnegie Mellon University students studying in the Master of Human-Computer Interaction program, which is under the School of Computer Science. To find out more about us and our client, go to About page.
Competitors
  • Historical preference as Liquidnet was first dark-pool
  • BIDS, ITG are cheaper, but give less info and data
  • This data is portfolio-related, like Discovery Analytics
Differences
  • Competitors ask for a lower commission (cheaper)
  • Liquidnet has the best liquidity as the first dark-pool
  • Competitors focus on algos and quick matches
Product and features
  • Individual products interact with each other regularly
  • Map of products to actions stressed common actions
  • Map for each trader type showed unique workflows
Workflows
  • Many workflows overlap features and products
  • Mapping shows relationship between trader actions
  • Active and passive trade workflows are most routine
Industry standards
  • Fintech software often emulates spreadsheets
  • Common colors are bright red, green and yellow
  • Industry standards are the same as a decade ago
Design standards
  • Many visual heuristics were not met by Liquidnet 5
  • Color and iconography will make use more intuitive
  • Visual hierarchy must guide trader through workflow
SYNTHESIS
Many traders already feel overwhelmed by their job, and their software doesn't help. Traders have cognitive and emotional overload from feature fear and difficult UI experiences.
Workflow overload
  • Traders typically master a workflow and stick to it
  • Connected workflows are not highlighted visually
  • Thus, traders are reluctant to learn new workflows
Finding features
  • Due to of visual clutter, new features are hard to find
  • No in-app help means traders must call sales for help
  • 'Hidden features' in blotter are unused and unseen
Juggling workflows
  • Traders have to juggle many workflows and data
  • Traders have to shift workflows based on market
  • Allowing the trader to change action should be easy
Finding liquidity
  • Traders inherently will use competing applications
  • Liquidity offerings can change trader's app usage
  • Liquidnet needs to convince traders to stay with them
Information overload
  • Poor visual design and hierarchy can confuse traders
  • If traders can't execute trades, they'll use competitor
  • Showing liquidity opportunity with info keeps trades
What's worth showing?
  • Screen real estate is extremely valuable and limited
  • What isn't seen isn't used or known to traders
  • When and what info is shown can convince trader to act
