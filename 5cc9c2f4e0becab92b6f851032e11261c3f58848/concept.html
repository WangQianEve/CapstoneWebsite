After conducting a variety of research activities and synthesizing findings, the team defined opportunity areas and began creating design concepts.

Core Concepts

Our Spring semester’s designs include low fidelity and middle fidelity screens for a potential next generation of our client’s software for traders. Here we describe some of the features:

1/ Flexible Layout

Traders have expressed a desire for customizable user interfaces that they can manipulate to reflect their specific trading styles or needs. In response to this, we suggest using tab-like segments of our client’s application that allows users to drag and drop menus into place in the user interface. Tabs will snap into place along a grid of different positions to allow for customization.

2/ User Profiles

Two items of note influenced our decision to pursue user profiles: firstly, our client expressed interest in measuring their user’s general behavior and application usage; and secondly, traders roughly participate in the same daily actions depending on their trader demographic. User profiles seek to aid both the client and the user: users develop profiles to allow customized responses to behavior, and clients gain data on trader groups to understand them better.

3/ Improved blotter and analytics

This concept is an iteration upon our client’s existing work with a blotter and pre-trade summary page, both of which are key data points for traders in the usage of our client’s product. Small changes to the design, like changing screen element sizes and using color and greyscale to categorize and define unseen information may significantly increase readability of these products.

4/ Team metrics for success

Traders regularly work in teams under a portfolio manager, and stay abreast of how the team is doing by verbally stating their actions to the team. Visualized team metrics and team behavior information can be essential to traders as they can figure out the team’s progress at a glance without asking for an update in-person, which takes time and is less accurate than a direct report.

5/ Customized alerts system

An essential part of the trading software ecosystem is alerts, as they are timely reminders or updates on the trades being conducted that day. Alerts have the potential to be more than alarming or annoying features; instead, we focus on giving the trader opportunities to develop their own alerts in type and also to selectively prioritize or deprioritize categories of alerts.

Process

Our team conducted a number of ideating activities that led to concrete ideas for low-fi and mid-fi prototypes, which we later evaluated by speed dating with our client and proxy users.

Ideating

The ideation phase involved two sketching activities, Crazy 8’s and storyboarding. Crazy 8’s focused on idea generation, while storyboards actualized the concepts that would be most valuable to our clients or aligned best with our focus areas.

Storyboard Examples

Crazy 8’s generated the concepts that were drawn into storyboards; for example,
the end-of-day report generation for peers was developed in Crazy 8’s and included
into storyboards alongside other concepts related to status and performance.

Concept Testing

After developing our storyboards, we performed a speed dating activity with our clients to evaluate its relevance and value to the client’s needs. Ideas of interest included customized menus and alternative information sharing functions.

Speed Dating

Concepts that were well received related to data processing and analytics, whereas
ideas around abstract concepts like closure were more difficult to grasp or
understand. Aspects of complex ideas, like reporting for closure, were rated well.

Initial Design Idea Testing

Ideas that successfully passed through speed dating were combined to create low fidelity prototypes, then refined into middle fidelity quality after consulting proxy users and clients.

Main Feedback

We received feedback not only on our own designs, but also queried proxy
users regarding their preferences in data visualization tools for trading.
In general, feedback fell into one of four categories: data analytics, team
communication and analysis, existing data visualizations and reporting.
Overall, users generally supported quick and accurate sharing of report
information and data analytics, which ties into our concept of closure as
well as focusing on better data representations (visualizations and analytics).

Iterating Prototype

After reflecting on the feedback from our testing, we iterated upon our low fidelity screens and developed secondary low fidelity and a first round of middle fidelity work.

To Be Continued...

Now that we have completed a number of middle fidelity works, we hope to continue iterating upon our design and present a series of concepts to our user’s clients during the month of June, after which we will incorporate user feedback to develop our final proposed designs and complete a set of high fidelity prototypes.
